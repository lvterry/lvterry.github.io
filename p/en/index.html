Career

My career as a designer started in 2006 when I made some CD cover and logo design in college. In 2007 I joined SAP as a UX Designer and stayed there for around 5 years. I learned all the basics of Design there. It was a great ride of learning and growing.

I've worked in different kinds of companies since then. Some are multi-national companies like Amazon and LinkedIn. Some are startups like alive.cn. Some are local private companies like Wuage and Huobi. I found different companies have different expectations for the role Designer. But no matter what teams I work in, I reject to simply follow orders of PMs. I need to think the problem through. I need to know who are the users and what goals they are trying to achieve in their life. I need to know whether our solution is the best we can do to help them.

My passion lies in creating good software. In all the teams I stayed, I managed to setup a working model that allows me to stand at the intersection of product management and design. I am grateful that all the partners had learned to appreciate my work in the end.

It all comes down to the definition of design. That always take me back to the Steve Jobs quote - "Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.”

Over the years, I've developed some design principles and been telling myself to stick to them. The measurement I use for good design is the fitness between the form and its context.

On top of that, great products should have personalities.
